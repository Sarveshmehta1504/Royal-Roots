Best Plywood for Your Kitchen

March 10, 2026
5 min read

The kitchen is the heart of the home, but for your furniture, it is practically a war zone. From boiling water and steam to heavy pots, hot oil spills, and rigorous cleaning—your kitchen cabinets undergo more stress than any other woodwork in your house.

Choosing the wrong plywood for a modular kitchen is the most common and expensive mistake homeowners make. Fortunately, it's an easy one to avoid.

The Danger of Humidity and Water

Kitchen base cabinets—especially the units beneath the sink—are constantly exposed to dampness, minor pluming leaks, and wet mops. If you use standard MR (Moisture Resistant) plywood or MDF here, the core layers will absorb the water. Within 12 to 18 months, the wood will swell, warp, and the hinges will pull out.

Why BWP (IS:710) is Non-Negotiable

For kitchen applications, BWP (Boiling Water Proof) plywood is the industry standard. Regulated by the IS:710 certification, BWP plywood is created using undiluted Phenol Formaldehyde resins. This creates an impenetrable waterproof bond between the core veneers.

Our Royal Roots Platinum Grade is the ultimate solution for modular kitchens. It features:

  • 100% Gurjan Core: Extremely dense and naturally resistant to borers.
  • IS:710 Certified: Fully boiling water proof.
  • Calibrated Thickness: Perfect for smooth, noiseless modular drawer systems.
"Using anything less than IS:710 under a kitchen sink is guaranteeing a remodel in three years."

A Smart Budgeting Strategy

If you have a strict budget, use the premium Platinum BWP grade for the base cabinets and sink units. For the overhead cabinets, which only face steam but no direct water, you can safely use our Royal Roots Gold Grade (Enhanced BWR 303). This strategic mixing of grades provides maximum longevity while optimizing costs.
