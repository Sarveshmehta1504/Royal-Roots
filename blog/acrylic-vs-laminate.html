Acrylic vs Laminate: Which Suits You?

March 10, 2026
6 min read

Once you've chosen high-quality Royal Roots IS:710 plywood for the core of your furniture, the next major decision is the surface finish. For modular kitchens, wardrobes, and living room units, the two most popular options are laminates and acrylic sheets.

Both materials offer distinct stylistic and practical advantages. In this guide, we break down the pros, cons, and ideal applications for both finishes to help you design a space that fits your lifestyle perfectly.

What is Acrylic Finish?

Acrylic is a non-toxic finish that offers a perfectly smooth texture and an ultra-high gloss appearance. It is reminiscent of sleek automotive paint or a perfectly smooth mirror.

Pros of Acrylic:

  • Stunning Aesthetics: Offers a premium, mirror-like gloss that reflects light, making small spaces feel significantly larger and brighter.
  • Vibrant Colors: Acrylic retains its color and brightness for years without fading from UV exposure.
  • Easy to Clean: The perfectly smooth surface can be wiped spotless with a microfiber cloth and mild cleaner.

Cons of Acrylic:

  • Maintenance Heavy: Fingerprints, smudges, and dirt are instantly visible on the high-gloss surface.
  • Prone to Scratches: It is relatively soft and can scratch easily if abrasive cleaners are used.
  • Higher Cost: Because of the manufacturing process, premium acrylic sheets are more expensive than laminates.

What is Laminate Finish?

Laminates (often called sunmica) are artificial materials made by pressing layers of paper and plastic resins together under high pressure. They come in both matte and gloss finishes, as well as heavily textured designs simulating raw stone or natural wood.

Pros of Laminates:

  • Incredible Durability: Laminates are highly resistant to scratches, wear, and tear, making them perfect for rough usage.
  • Endless Variety: Available in matte, high-gloss, and deeply textured mimicking wood grain, marble, or fabric.
  • Cost-Effective: Very budget-friendly compared to acrylics while still providing great longevity.

Cons of Laminates:

  • Less Reflective: Even high-gloss laminates cannot match the mirror-like smoothness of premium acrylic.
  • Edge Banding Constraints: Over time, the edges of laminates can chip if not properly finished with PVC edge banding.

The Final Verdict: Where to Use Which?

A smart, modern design strategy often involves mixing both materials to balance aesthetics, budget, and durability!

For Modular Kitchens:

Use Acrylic on the upper cabinets to reflect light, draw the eye upward, and create a premium feel. Use Laminate on the lower base cabinets, as these face more wear and tear, pet scratches, and frequent cleaning.
